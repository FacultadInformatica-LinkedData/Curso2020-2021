Dataset requirements document

Requirements satisfied discussion

The datasets chosen involves the city of Madrid and its air quality for the past decade. The following list contains the discussion on whether the datasets satisfy the requirements or not:

  1. R1. Data are in one of the selected smart city domains:

    The datasets are indeed from the city of Madrid.

  2. R2. Data are available as a CSV file:

    The data could be pulled from .txt, .xml or .csv formats.

  3. R3. Data have an open license so they can be published:

    The rightsholder of the data (the city council of Madrid) published the data under the Condiciones de Uso, that ensures the use and production of the data, as long as the source is quoted.

  4. R4. Data can be easily linked with generic real-world entities (e.g., locations):

    The data contains geolocatable entities, such as sampling points, that can be linked to locations in the real world. Indeed, that is one of the desired requirements for the built-in application.

  5. R5. Documentation exists for the data (optional):

    There is documentation that join the data, that allows to understand the codes and the meaning of every column of the dataset. Link here.

  6. R6. Data can come from multiple data sources (optional):

    Our data come from different sources (2010-February 2020 // March 2020 onwards).
